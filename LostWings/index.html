RESCUE SUPPORT

Our role in rescuing

This is how we support or help the rescue team to find the lost aircraft and save the valuable lives.

01

Accurate Location Tracking

We provide possible search areas precisely based on the last known location, geographical coordinates, departure and arrival times, and flight paths. This enables rescue teams to focus their efforts on the most promising locations, significantly improving the chances of a successful recovery.

02

Real-Time Updates and Coordination

Our platform offers real-time updates and coordinates with rescue teams to ensure they have the most current information. This helps in adjusting search plans swiftly and accurately, enhancing the efficiency of rescue operations.

03

Advanced Mapping and Visualization Tools

Utilizing state-of-the-art mapping and visualization tools, we create detailed maps of possible aircraft locations. These tools allow rescue teams to visualize the search area clearly, making it easier to plan and execute search and rescue missions effectively.

Phil Bradley

"I don't know what happened, but I have no fear." Sole survivor in the 1959 crash of Piedmont Airlines Flight 349 near Crozet, Virginia.

SFO John Coward

"As the final approach started I became aware that there was no power ... suddenly there was nothing from any of the engines, and the plane started to glide." British Airways flight 38 crash on 17 January 2008.

Ashiq Perumbal

"The Air India plane was out of control. We felt it breaking into two. I thought I had lost my hands". One of the survivors of the Air India plane crash On 7 August 2020, in Kozhikode, India.

Manish Shakya

On 24 July 2024, a Bombardier CRJ200ER operated by Saurya Airlines crashed shortly after take-off at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, Nepal. Manish Shakya was the sole survivor.
